Project Idea : Performance Tracking Glasses

Overview

While there are precarious amounts of fitness and performance tracking apps for phones and smart watches, all these require the individual to stop what they're doing and monitor their performance. This can be detrimental to people who compete, or practice endurance training such as running and cycling. So, I believe that there should be an alternative that not only allows these individuals the capability to track their performance in real time, but view their current performance in contrast to those around them and they’re own previous records. I call this project HUDFitness, which involves the planning and creation of a set of digital glasses that connect to you phone or smart watch, displaying the info that the individual may find useful during their session, informing the user in real time of things such as their pace, performance and heart rate just name a few.

Motivation

This project idea, although cliché in this current market offers originality in the sense that it has the ability to act as a performance assistant in Realtime, providing insight to the estimated 48 million cyclist and 47 million runners in the US alone. These HUDFitness glasses would also require minimal pre-existing tools for function as it would rely on Bluetooth connectivity to basic items that people carry on them such as phones or even smart watches. My personal motivation for its eventual creation is due my wish that I can view and monitor my own welfare whilst working out in Realtime, providing me insight into the little details such as if I'm pushing too hard or not enough and if I'm acting within my optimum range.

Description

These HUDFitness glasses will serve a variety of hardware and software functions ranging from tracking individual stats to displaying the next track on a playlist within the digital display of the glasses. The HUD will also allow users to access ALL the apps that they normally would on their phone or smart watch through its Bluetooth connectivity, meaning that the user can still rely on their phone or smartwatch to plan a route or goal distance prior to starting, and rely on the glasses to display something such as maps or even the estimated time to reach the goal based on previous runs. The glasses would also be made to have the ability to output two different displays across the 2 lenses, allowing the user to further personalize the way they monitor their performance, for example, showing their heartrate and speed on one lens and the map with distance if they so desire to on the other. Similarly, to other fitness tracking apps like STRAVA (very good app that works on phone) the glasses would be accompanied by an app that can be installed alongside the purchase of the glasses. This app would act as a personal data bank to everything the user may want to track, such as performance and speed along a section of track over a few months, or even record the average speed the user managed to produce during their chosen endurance activity. To further bolster the glasses originality, I would aim to implement a Bluetooth speaker/earphone system that can connect the individuals music, allowing them to listen to music and track their performance all in one connection, rather than deciding what would be more important for the cycle/run(Bluetooth can only have one device connect at a single time). Moreover, the brightness and intensity of the display is fully up to the user, meaning that the opacity of the display can be adjusted to the user's eyes, and the time of day. As a side function, the glasses will not only display the UI set by the user but it will also protect the users eyes from the elements and they complete their physical activities.

Tools and Technology

While I ascertain that a product such as this doesn’t require much in terms of hardware tech to work functionally, I do believe that a project such as this would require intensive programming and networking to not only produce the outputs on the screens, but also allow the user to interface with the display of specific app and icons on the screens. However, I do feel as if though an amazing open source tool that would be able to assist me in the creation of a product such as this would be Trello. As it allows a user to create, moderate, and organize the completion of milestones and goals.

Skills Required

  • Prerequisite Knowledge

To produce the code to a functional level, I’ll need to have a knowledgeable understanding of java programming (for android) and swift for IOS if I wish to create the application which stores the user's information regarding their activity, records and times.

  • Software Requirements/ Needs

Access to Objective-C and java programming launchers, such as eclipse for java and swift for coding in Android and IOS respectively.

  • Required Hardware

Not much hardware is needed besides a frame for the glasses, wireless earphone tech and UI display glass for the front, which should be relatively straight forward to find or purchase. However, time would be needed to solve how the HUDFitness glasses will be assembled and time will be needed for the actual assembly of the product.

Outcome

If the project were to be successful, this item could be marketed to everyone who practices endurance fitness, regardless if they are just cycling for fun, or even attempting to prepare for an upcoming event. And while the problem isn't much more than a minor inconvenience, it still allows the user to adapt their pace and performance in real time to break barriers and records, with having to stop midway, or ponder the whole time, seeing only if you managed the goal at the end. if something like this were to take of successfully, this product could be updated to take into account weather and conditions, and measure everything without the requirement of pairing the HUDFitness glasses with a phone or smart device.
